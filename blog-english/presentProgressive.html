Present Progressive Tense

Uses of the present progressive

  • Actions that happening at the moment of speaking (now, at the moment)
  • Temporaly situations
  • Future arrangements

Grammar Structure

Affirmative Subject + Vbe + Ving + Complement
Negative Subject + Vbe + Not + Ving + Complement
Question Wh + Vbe + Subject + Ving + Complement + ?

Time expressions

  • Listen
  • Look
  • Quiet
  • Now
  • Right now
  • At the moment
  • Tomorrow
  • Tonight
  • next week
  • This - There

Rules

There are spelling variations with the verbs ending in:

  1. "consonant + e" we cut the "e" and add "ing"
  2. "vowel + consonant" we double the consonant and add "ing"

Examples

Affirmative

  1. We're studying English
  2. They're working now
  3. We're going to the cinema later
  4. I'am living in San Jose

Negative

  1. We aren't studying English
  2. They aren't working now
  3. We aren't going to the cinema later
  4. I'am not living in San Jose

Interrogative

  1. Are we studying English?
  2. Are they working now?
  3. Are we going to the cinema later
  4. Am I living in San Jose

Past Simple Tense

Uses of the past simple

  • Actions finished in the past.
  • Series of completed actions in the past.
  • Together with the Past Progreesive – the Simple past interrupted an action which was in progress in the past.

Common expressions of time in past

  • Yesterday
  • Last week
  • A month ago
  • In 2003

Verbs in Past

Regular verbs

  • Verbs in past can be formed adding “ed” with most verbs
    • Example: Start => started
    • Example: Kill => killed
    • Example> Jump => jumped
  • If the verb ends in “e” you only add “d”
    • Example: Agree => agreed
    • Example: Like => liked
    • Example> Escape => escaped
  • If a verb ends in a vowel and a consonant, the consonant is usually doubled before -ed.
    • Example: Stop → stopped
    • Example: Plan → planned
  • If a verb ends in consonant and -y, you take off the y and add -ied.
    • Example: Try → tried
    • Example: Carry → carried
  • But if the word ends in a vowel and -y, you add -ed.
    • Example: play → played
    • Example: enjoy → enjoyed

Irregular verbs

There are a lot of verbs, but the most important is the verb to be

Verb ToBe in past

Was

  • I
  • He
  • She
  • It

Were

  • They
  • You
  • We

Grammar Structure

Affirmative Subject + VerbPast + Complement
Negative Subject + VerbPast + not + Complement
Question Verbpast + Subject + Complement + ?

Examples of simple past using verb to be

Affirmative

  1. I was in my mother’s house yesterday
  2. They were friends in the high school
  3. She was in classes the last week

Negative

  1. I wasn’t in my mother’s house yesterday
  2. They weren’t friends in the high school
  3. She wasn’t in classes the las week

Interrogative

  1. Was I in my mother’s house yesterday?
  2. Were they friends in the high school
  3. Was she in classes the last week

Past Simple with out verb ToBe

Grammar Structure

Affirmative Subject + VerbPast + Complement
Negative Subject + did + not + VerbSimple + Complement
Question Wh + did + Subject + VerbSimple + Complement + ?

Affirmative

  1. They arrived late for class
  2. He went to school yesterday

Negative

  1. They didn't arrive late for class
  2. He didn't go to school yesterday

Interrogative

  1. Did they arrive late for class?
  2. Did he go to school yesterday?
  3. Do we have time?
